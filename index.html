Key observations
Brazil: from 94 → 6,701 registrations
Q1 2025 had only 94 Brazil registrations — Brazil was effectively offline. Q1 2026 saw a 71× surge driven by BlackArrow (+8,815%) and App (+6,761%). This explains why the registration base is strong but 2025 YoY comparisons look artificially large.
Korea: -41% YoY — registration base is shrinking
Korea MT4 fell from 788 → 307 (-61%) and MT5 from 721 → 472 (-35%). Korea's FTD avg ticket ($17,023) remains the highest in the business, but fewer new users entering the funnel is a structural concern for future quarters. Only App is growing (+54%).
Monthly deceleration needs watching
Jan 5,663 → Feb 3,495 → Mar 2,560: down 55% from January peak to March. Some of this is normal post-launch seasonality, but the trend needs monitoring in April. If March pace continues, Q2 registrations would land around 7,680 — a 35% QoQ decline.

Platform observations
BlackArrow dominates in FTD volume, not avg ticket
BlackArrow drives 57.7% of all FTDs (1,655 users) but only $3,422 avg ticket. MT4 is the opposite — 7.9% of FTDs but $8,190 avg ticket, the highest of any platform. MT5 at $6,869 is also significantly ahead of BlackArrow. Volume ≠ value here.
Korea APP: 18 FTDs, $3,944 avg — clear opportunity
Korea MT5 avg ticket is $20,126 and MT4 is $13,193 — the highest in the entire dataset. Korea APP users average $3,944 but there are only 18 of them. A targeted campaign to move Korean desktop traders to the app could yield disproportionate returns.
APP avg ticket ($1,561) is 5× below MT4 — structural gap
APP platform: 353 FTDs, $1,561 avg ticket. MT4: 226 FTDs, $8,190 avg. The app is acquiring more users but at much lower deposit value. Whether this is a UX issue, audience mix, or product limitation needs investigation before scaling app UA.
